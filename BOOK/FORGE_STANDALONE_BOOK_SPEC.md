## CHAPTER 1 OPENING — THE NON-TECH CROWD

Tone: Sarcastic. Direct. Personal.

The high-tech industry is maybe missing a small but powerful market. The non-tech crowd. Roughly 95% of users.

In the beginning you do not need to know how to prompt. You literally login and ask a question. That is it. And it works. AI answers you. You are hooked.

But then your questions get more complex. Now you are chasing answers. You asked one question and got a response that was close but not quite right. So you ask a follow-up. Then another. Then another. Six, seven, eight questions deep and you are either frustrated because it did not tell you what you expected, or you got something useful but you are not sure if you asked it correctly.

AI may eventually be able to ask you the first question of the day. But for now, you have to go first. You have to ask the first question. And then stay on task and ask the second. And the third. And so on until you think you have what you needed to get started.

Now stop. Look back at those 6-8 questions you asked. Group them together in your mind. You asked 7 questions? Out of those 7, you could categorize them. Some were about scope. Some were about format. Some were about constraints you did not even know you had until the AI gave you the wrong thing.

Break down the list. Organize the direction path. Now look at what you have.

You can see something that looks like a prompt.

You just built one. Without a framework. Without an acronym. Without a $10K course. You did what humans do naturally: you asked questions until you understood what you actually needed.

That is pretty much all you need to do. Just ask questions.

Once you are comfortable in your ask sequence, once you recognize the pattern in your own behavior, NOW is when FORGE helps you move to the next level. FORGE is structured to teach a different perspective on prompting. It teaches an approach that refines your ask and then tells you why it did so. It does not replace your instinct. It formalizes it.

The tech industry built tools for the 5% who already speak the language. FORGE is the equalizer for the other 95%.

## Three-Tier Protocol (every test case)
- Tier 1: Vanilla (raw prompt, standard LLM, full unedited response captured and shown)
- Tier 2: FORGE (ANVIL extraction + ASSAY scoring + execution, full response shown)
- Tier 3: FORGE+METATRON (protocol governance + CIL multi-agent, full response shown)

## ASSAY Formula
Score = (Specificity x 0.25) + (Completeness x 0.25) + ((10-HallucinationRisk) x 0.20) + (OutputClarity x 0.20) + (NegativeConstraints x 0.10)
