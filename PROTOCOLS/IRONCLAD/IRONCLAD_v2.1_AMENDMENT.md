## 1. PROBLEM STATEMENT

The flat -5% stop-loss applied uniformly across all position types has resulted in repeated whipsaw exits during elevated volatility (VIX 20+), converting temporary drawdowns into permanent realized losses. In a market averaging 1.4% daily swings, a -5% stop triggers on normal volatility rather than thesis failure. The cumulative effect is identical to a single large loss -- the money is gone, just slower. This amendment replaces the uniform stop with a tiered matrix calibrated to each ring's volatility profile and holding period.

Additionally, trim discipline was inconsistent because no distinction existed between short-duration tactical plays and structural thesis holds. The March 16 update introduces mandatory Track Assignment at entry and a 3/5/8 EOD stagger for Track 1 positions, enforced at 3:45 PM daily.

## 4. THE PROCESS -- NON-NEGOTIABLE SEQUENCE

### At Entry:
1. Declare Track 1 or Track 2
2. Set stop immediately per IRONCLAD tiered matrix
3. Set 3:45 PM calendar alert (Track 1 only)

### At 3:45 PM Daily (Track 1 positions):
1. Check all Track 1 positions
2. Calculate % gain from cost basis
3. Apply trim matrix -- no debate, no "let it run"
4. Place sell order before 4 PM close
5. Cash goes to purchasing power -- not redeployed same day

### At Session Close:
1. Log the trim in trade journal
2. Update position size
3. Note remaining shares and new effective cost basis
