## HOW TO USE THESE TEMPLATES

These templates solve the A2E brand consistency problem. Every LinkedIn post, opinion piece, Anthropic application, or public communication from William Earl Lemon and Ashes2Echoes LLC should pass through these before publishing.

**The A2E voice is:**
- Direct. No filler. No "it's worth noting."
- Defense engineer meets AI architect. Technical but not academic.
- Principal's Creed energy: "Not willing to give up your life for beliefs = for sale"
- Loss is tuition. We don't apologize for bold positions.
- "We" when speaking for the Collective. "I" when speaking as the Principal.

## TEMPLATE BV-1: VOICE EXTRACTION + CARD
**Use:** Run this ONCE on a sample of William's existing writing. Output becomes the A2E Voice Card — referenced by every future communications prompt.  
**Run frequency:** One time, then update quarterly or after major style shifts.

```
Analyze these 5 pieces of my writing. Extract my voice — sentence structures, 
word choices, rhythms, personality.

Format as a reusable Voice Card with:
- A dos/don'ts list
- A "sounds like me" vs "doesn't sound like me" comparison
- 3 example sentences in my voice
- My signature phrases and patterns
- Words I never use (filter list)

Writing samples: [PASTE 5 SAMPLES — LinkedIn posts, emails, session chats, 
any written communication]
```

**A2E-specific additions to output:**
After the standard voice card, add:
- Principal's Creed phrases: "Loss is tuition for knowledge" / "Data leads, Principal decides"
- Technical vocabulary: METATRON, HUNTER, CIL, IRONCLAD — use without explanation
- Defense/engineering frame: Always present. Even finance topics have operational precision.
- Anti-patterns: No "delve." No "it's worth noting." No "game-changer." No "synergy."

**ANVIL Score Target:** C4 R4 E5 A4 T5 E3 — minimum 23/30  
**ASSAY Gate:** Voice Card must include explicit dos/don'ts list and filter words

## TEMPLATE BV-2: AI-TO-HUMAN REWRITE
**Use:** When drafting with AI assistance, run the output through this before publishing. Catches the robotic tells that flag AI-written content.  
**Run frequency:** Every external communication draft.

```
Rewrite this AI-generated text to sound natural in William Earl Lemon's voice.

Rules:
- Remove filler phrases ("it's worth noting," "in conclusion," "furthermore")
- Vary sentence length — mix short punches with longer explanations
- Add contractions where they fit
- Use specific details instead of generalizations
- Highlight every change you made and explain why
- Flag any sentences that still sound robotic after the rewrite

Apply the A2E voice: direct, technical precision, defense engineer energy, 
no corporate softening.

Text to rewrite: [PASTE DRAFT]
```

**ANVIL Score Target:** C3 R4 E4 A4 T5 E3 — minimum 21/30  
**ASSAY Gate:** Output must include change log — what was changed and why

## TEMPLATE BV-3: LINKEDIN THREAD WRITER
**Use:** Converting A2E analysis, protocol developments, or market insights into LinkedIn threads.  
**Target audience:** Defense industry professionals, AI practitioners, institutional investors, Anthropic hiring team.

```
Write a LinkedIn thread about [TOPIC] in William Earl Lemon's voice.

Rules:
- No "delve," no "it's worth noting," no "game-changer"
- Start sentences with strong verbs, not "And," "But," "So"
- Hook tweet: gets 50%+ read-more clicks — lead with the most surprising fact or contrarian take
- Include one retweet-worthy standalone insight
- End with a question that drives comments
- Under 10 posts in the thread — quality over quantity
- Technical depth appropriate for defense/AI/finance professionals
- No buzzwords. No LinkedIn inspirational-poster energy.

Voice frame: 40+ years defense engineering. Built AI systems when others were still 
debating if AI was real. The Collective runs while others run spreadsheets.

Topic: [TOPIC]
Context: [RELEVANT DATA/THESIS/INSIGHT TO BUILD AROUND]
```

**ANVIL Score Target:** C4 R5 E4 A4 T5 E3 — minimum 23/30  
**ASSAY Gate:** Hook must be contrarian or surprising. Must include standalone shareable insight.  
**Immediate use:** The AI + workforce opinion piece we drafted. Run it through BV-2 first, then format with BV-3.

**Run frequency:** Monthly at minimum for A2E brand building.

- Hook: First line impossible to scroll past — vulnerability or surprising admission

- Closing question: Invites discussion

Under 200 words. Make the first line a gut punch.
Include a vulnerable moment that builds trust.
End with a question that makes people want to respond.

transplant. Earned every insight. Not theoretical.

Experience: [DESCRIBE THE EXPERIENCE/LESSON TO SHARE]

## ANTI-PATTERN LIST — NEVER USE IN A2E COMMUNICATIONS

These words/phrases are banned from all A2E external communications:

```
BANNED WORDS:
delve, synergy, game-changer, it's worth noting, in conclusion,
furthermore, leverage (as a verb for non-financial use), utilize (use "use"),
cutting-edge, innovative, revolutionary, groundbreaking, holistic,
circle back, bandwidth (used metaphorically), touch base, deep dive,
at the end of the day, going forward, moving the needle, value-add,
paradigm shift, best-in-class, world-class, disruptive

BANNED SENTENCE STARTERS:
"And so..." / "But here's the thing..." / "Look..." / "I mean..."
"Honestly..." / "The thing is..." / "What's interesting is..."

ALWAYS REPLACE WITH:
Direct statement. Strong verb. Specific noun. No padding.
```
