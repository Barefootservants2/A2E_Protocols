## PART IV: AUTHORITY SCORING FORMULA

### Source Quality Quantification

```
AS = (PT × RW × EM × RS) / BF

Where:
PT = Publication Tier (1-5)
RW = Recency Weight (1.0 - 0.1 decay)
EM = Expertise Match (0-1)
RS = Reproducibility Score (0-1)
BF = Bias Factor (1.0 - 2.0)

Minimum Threshold: AS ≥ 2.0
```

**Publication Tier Examples:**
- Tier 5: Peer-reviewed journal, official government source
- Tier 4: Primary company source (SEC filings, official PR)
- Tier 3: Major news outlet with named sources
- Tier 2: Industry publication, analyst report
- Tier 1: Blog, forum, unverified claim

**This forces citation quality, not just citation presence.**

## PART V: COUNTER-THESIS GENERATION (Gate 7.5)

### Mandatory Adversarial Review

For every thesis, the system must generate minimum 3 failure modes:

| Category | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| MARKET RISK | Macro/sector-level threat | Interest rate spike kills growth thesis |
| DOMAIN RISK | Specific-entity threat | Company loses key contract |
| THESIS RISK | Core assumption failure | Underlying premise is wrong |

**Why This Matters:**

Current AI systems are "yes machines" — they find evidence supporting user premises. Gate 7.5 forces the system to actively seek disconfirming evidence.

**Enterprise Application:**
- Manufacturing: "This production plan will fail if..."
- Engineering: "This design will fail when..."
- Finance: "This investment thesis breaks if..."
- Legal: "This argument collapses when..."

### Manufacturing Use Case: CAD to Production

**Input:** CAD model, material specs, tolerance requirements, production volume

**METATRON Protocol Execution:**

1. **Gate 0.5 (Premise Challenge):** Verify material specs are current, tolerances are achievable
2. **Gate 1 (RAG):** Pull similar production runs, machine capabilities, supplier data
3. **Gate 2 (Authority):** Score sources — manufacturer specs > generic databases
4. **Gate 5.5 (Catalyst):** Check for recent material availability changes, pricing shifts
5. **Gate 6 (Consensus):** Cross-reference multiple production planning methods
6. **Gate 7 (Confidence):** Provide ranges for cycle time, yield, cost
7. **Gate 7.5 (Counter-Thesis):** 
   - Market: Supply chain disruption scenarios
   - Domain: Machine capability edge cases
   - Thesis: Material substitution requirements
8. **Gate 8 (Methodology):** Full production plan with decision log
9. **Gate 8.5 (Regulatory):** Compliance check for target market
10. **Gate 11 (HUNTER):** Scan for optimization opportunities

**Output:** Production plan with confidence intervals, failure modes, compliance verification, and audit trail.
