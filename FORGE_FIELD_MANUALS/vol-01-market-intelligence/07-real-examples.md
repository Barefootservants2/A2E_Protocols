## Example 7.3 — The Semiconductor Supply Chain Miss (PROBE Grade)

**Context:** Strait of Hormuz closure disrupted helium supply from Ras Laffan, Qatar. Helium is critical for semiconductor fabrication. The thesis chain from SK Hynix → TSMC → SMH should have been identified before the disruption, not after.

**Why this is a PROBE, not a PRIME:** The analysis was structurally sound but late. The supply chain mapping prompts that would have caught this dependency did not exist yet. This failure directly led to the creation of Chain 3.2 (Supply Chain Disruption Chain).

**Lesson:** A framework that cannot detect what it doesn't know it should be looking for is incomplete. FORGE's value is not just in the prompts it contains but in the systematic process of adding new prompts when failures reveal gaps.
